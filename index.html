Freydís Eiríksdóttir

Freydis born in Iceland around 970.
Daughter of the famous Erik Thorvaldsson called Erik the Red.
She led several expeditions on the lands of Vinland (northeastern Canada today), where she terrorized the Skrælingjar in Icelandic (Amerindian people).
Freydís is a perfect example of the integration of women into Scandinavian society.

Harald Sigurdsson


Son of Sigurd Syr, one of the kings of the Kingdom of Ringerike in Norway, Harald lives by day around 1015.
Better known as Harald Hardrada, he is often considered to be the last king of the Viking era.
Following a revolt, he first went into exile in Kiev's Rus', then to Constantinople where he became head of the Varangian guard.
Around 1045, he returned to Norway and acquired the title of King of Norway.
His last battle was on September 25, 1066 at Stamford Bridge near York, in the Saxon kingdom.
He was a great genius of war whose feats of arms will inspire many of today's historical reconstructions.
